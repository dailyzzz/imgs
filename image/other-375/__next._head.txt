HISTORICAL CONTEXT - Operation High Jump: Real US Navy operation conducted December 1946 to April 1947 (Antarctic summer when conditions allow access). Largest Antarctic expedition ever attempted at that time - involved 4,700 personnel, 13 ships, 33 aircraft. Led by Rear Admiral Richard E. Byrd, famous polar explorer who had previously flown over South Pole in 1929. Official objectives: establish Antarctic research base (Little America IV), train personnel in polar operations, test equipment in extreme cold, conduct scientific research, map coastline and interior regions. Operation included aerial photography mapping 60% of Antarctic coastline - produced first comprehensive maps of continent. Mission also had military aspects: testing cold weather equipment and tactics during early Cold War period when polar routes were strategic concern. Operation established US presence in Antarctica ahead of anticipated territorial claims by other nations. Mission was public, well-documented, and reported by embedded journalists. Results included extensive aerial photography, geographic discoveries, equipment testing data, and establishment of US Antarctic research program that continues through McMurdo Station and other bases.

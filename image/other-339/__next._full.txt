What remained was Apollo spacecraft consisting of: Command Module (CM - crew cabin for 3 astronauts), Service Module (SM - propulsion, power, life support), and Lunar Module (LM - lunar lander). Only CM returned to Earth - SM separated just before reentry. So "transformation" was actually systematic discarding of components: Rocket stages → Apollo spacecraft → LM on moon → LM ascent stage → CM capsule. This is mass ratio principle: most of rocket is fuel to accelerate small payload. Mission used 6.5 million pounds fully fueled to return 12,000-pound CM to Earth. Meme's arrows suggest mysterious transformation rather than progressive jettisoning of expended components. This misunderstanding common among those unfamiliar with spaceflight principles. Every rocket works this way - SpaceX Falcon 9 is 230 feet tall but carries payloads of few tons.
